## Flavian Amphitheater - Colliseum

podium - raised ring that separates arena from audience (cavea)
arena - "sand" in latin
longitudonal axis - player's entry
cross axis - emperor's seat
 - barbarians and emperors in the same space
opposite the emperor was the editor, who ran games in emporer's absense

seating organized by class; Senator, Middle, Low, Slave/Foreign, Women

many forms of combat, grew from Forum to Circus to dedicated Arena

Venatio - the hunt
Damnatio - convicts released into arena against threat, if they survive they go free
- damnatio ad bestias (lion, etc)
- damnatio ad gladius (sword, archer)
many sentences read out, one sword tossed, last man standing goes free
bodies would be taken to rot on the steps of the temple of mars
Munera - usually proffessional gladiators, well known, often lived well if briefly
